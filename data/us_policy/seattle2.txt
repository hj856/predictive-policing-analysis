SPD Rolls Out Predictive Policing Software
by Jonah Spangenthal-Lee on February 27, 2013 4:52 pm
Mayor Mike McGinn and Seattle Police Chief John Diaz unveiled SPD’s new Predictive Policing software at a press conference today.



The software allows SPD to do a deep analysis of past crime data, and forecast possible locations where crimes might occur in the city, down to a 500-foot geographic area.

With that information in hand, officers can spend more time pro-actively patrolling those areas when they’re not responding to 911 calls.

SPD has already deployed the new Predictive Policing software—which is part of the 20/20 policing initiative—in the East and Southwest Precincts, and should be in place in the rest of the department’s precincts by April 2013.
